!DOCTYPE Label

> It's use to specify what type of html doc the sever it's gonna read. Usually it's written 'html' after !DOCTYPE due to the ! which mean that this label own an atribute

The Hierarchy in the webpage

> Every webpage it's divided by two parts.

HEAD

> Which it's conformed by the *TITLE* of the wpage and the *URL* which contains foundamental settings for the entire wpage.

HTML

> This is the content of the entire page such as body, pictures, vids, hyperlinks and other stuff.

So...

For that reason of hierarchy, it's highly recommended to follow the *html labels* and structure

HTML Structure:

  • !DOCTYPE html
  • html
    • head
      • label for head exa: title
    • /head
    • body
      • label for body exa: h1, p
    • /body
  • /html

- Attributes

lang=""

> This attribute it's use to indicate what *language* belongs to the wpage. This has many advantages like to be easily found in a country depending on its *language*.

Also... this attribute it's used in the *html* label. exa:

----- html lang="en"

----- lang = language

----- "en" = english (it could be spanish)

META label

> This label doesn't have something inside it because this doesn't have another pair to *close* the instruction. > Normally it's used to *approach* its *_attributes_* which *let to give more information* about our web page. > Meta label it's not neccessary to close with a / before the >.

Some of the used attributes:

----- name="description / autor / keywords" content="(info to show)",

----- charset="",

----- and others...
